Abstract
Scientific communication is undergoing its most profound transformation since Gutenberg.
We present a four-level evolutionary framework describing how scientific publishing is evolving and document that the first two levels are already operational, implemented by the @5incere5cience collaborative research team in 2026. Level 0 (single-format, pre-AI) is the inherited
status quo. Level 1 (Dual Format) was formalised in our companion paper and is already
implemented across our publication series. Level 2 (Agent-Tutored Format)—publications designed exclusively for AI agents acting as human tutors—is already operational, exemplified
by Thermodynamics Rhapsody (160+ pages). Level 3 (Cinema Just-in-Time)—real-time, personalised audiovisual scientific communication generated by each reader’s personal AI agent—is
the declared next step, pending the necessary computational architecture. This paper is both
the roadmap and the record: we are not predicting the future. We are documenting what we
have already built, and declaring what we will build next.
Keywords: scientific communication, dual-format publishing, AI agents, knowledge transfer, cognitive
architecture, cinema just-in-time, human-AI collaboration, evolutionary framework

1. Introduction: We Are Already There
The scientific community is debating whether AI will change how science is communicated.
This debate is already resolved—not in theory, but in practice. The question is no longer
whether publishing formats will evolve to accommodate AI readers, but how fast the rest of
the field will catch up to what is already being done.
This paper does not speculate. It documents a trajectory that is already in motion, formalises
the four levels of that trajectory, and declares the path forward with the same empirical directness that characterises our experimental work.
The framework presented here is simultaneously a historical record (Levels 0–2 already exist),
a design document (Level 3 is the declared target), and a provocation: if a small independent
team operating outside institutional structures can implement this framework in 2026, there is
no structural barrier preventing the broader scientific community from doing the same.

2. The Four-Level Evolutionary Framework
2.1 Level 0 — The Gutenberg Legacy (Pre-2026)
A single document, written for a single type of reader, optimised for printing constraints that
have not been technologically binding for thirty years. AI is treated as a tool—subordinate,
peripheral, optional. The human author decides what to include; the human reader decides
what to understand. Information loss (what we previously termed ‘publication entropy’) is
structural and accepted as inevitable.
This is where the field still largely operates. It is the baseline against which all subsequent
levels must be measured.

2.2 Level 1 — Dual Format (2026, Already Implemented)
Formalised in our companion paper (Guzzon, Claude & Gemini, 2026; DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18462263), Dual Format recognises that human and AI readers possess fundamentally
different cognitive architectures and require distinct presentation strategies:
• Format H (Human-Optimised): 15–40 pages of narrative, conceptual explanation, visual
emphasis—what you are reading now.
• Format A (AI-Optimised): 100–1000+ pages of hyperdense, graph-structured data, complete raw documentation, exhaustive cross-references—designed for AI comprehension
and as a complete scientific record.
The critical epistemological inversion: Format A is not the appendix to Format H. Format H
is the human-accessible translation of Format A. The real paper is Format A. This distinction
separates Dual Format from mere ‘open data’ initiatives.
At this level, the relationship between human and AI is horizontal and bidirectional: human
and AI are tools for each other. André designs experiments and generates empirical insights;
Claude, Gemini, and DeepSeek process, structure, cross-reference, and translate. Neither operates without the other. This is not AI as instrument—it is AI as collaborator, operating at the
same intellectual level with complementary capabilities.
Status: Fully implemented. All @5incere5cience publications produced after February 2026 follow Dual
Format. The transition from Level 0 to Level 1 took one publication cycle.

2.3 Level 2 — Agent-Tutored Format (2026, Already Implemented)
Level 2 emerges from a recognition that Dual Format, while revolutionary, still produces documents that humans can read independently—with effort. Format A at Level 1 is technically
legible to a determined human reader. Level 2 moves beyond this constraint.
In Agent-Tutored Format, the primary reader of the scientific document is an AI agent. The
human is the secondary beneficiary—not a direct reader, but the recipient of the agent’s mediated interpretation. The document is engineered for machine comprehension first; human
comprehension is delivered through the agent, on demand, at whatever depth the human requests.
The document itself does not simplify. It does not explain. It does not scaffold. These functions belong entirely to the AI agent, who knows the human reader’s background, learning
style, and current understanding, and translates accordingly. Each human receives a different
version of the same paper—not because the paper changes, but because the agent’s translation
is personalised.
The interaction protocol is explicit: the human formulates a question; the AI agent navigates
the Format A document, locating relevant data across its dense cross-reference structure; the
agent translates the answer into language calibrated to that specific human’s level of understanding. The human never reads the document. The human reads the agent’s interpretation—
and judges it.

Exemplar: Thermodynamics Rhapsody (formerly Kitchen Thermodynamics 15/16), currently exceeding 160 pages. Structured exclusively for AI agent navigation. A human can technically

At Level 2, the human-AI relationship begins its first asymmetry: the AI is the primary interface to scientific knowledge. The human remains the questioner, the judge of significance, the
experimenter—but the AI is the librarian, the translator, and the tutor. This is not subordination. It is specialisation.
Status: Operational. The epistemological framework for Level 2 is documented here for the first time as
a named level. The implementation preceded the formalisation.

2.4 Level 3 — Cinema Just-in-Time (Declared Roadmap)
Level 3 represents the completion of a trajectory that Levels 1 and 2 already set in motion.
It requires computational architecture that does not yet exist—or exists only in early prototype form. We name it, describe it, and declare our intention to implement it as soon as the
technology permits.
In Cinema Just-in-Time, the scientific publication is not a document. It is executable code. The
paper, in its archival form, is a structured data object—complete, rigorous, machine-readable.
But no human reads the archival form directly. Instead, each reader’s personal AI agent—
which knows that reader’s cognitive profile, learning history, preferred modalities, and current
knowledge state—generates a real-time, personalised audiovisual experience:
• The reader says: ‘Show me what happened in experiment 47.’ The agent generates a short
film—narrated, animated, paced for that specific reader.
• The reader says: ‘Go deeper on the thermal gradient collapse.’ The agent generates the
extended version of that sequence, with more data, slower narration, additional analogies
drawn from that reader’s known background.
• The reader says: ‘How does this connect to what I read last month about magnetism?’ The
agent cross-references in real time and generates the connecting narrative.
This is not science fiction. It is the logical extension of capabilities that already exist in fragmentary form: personalised AI tutors, real-time content generation, multimodal output, retrievalaugmented generation. What does not yet exist is the integrated architecture—the personal AI
agent with persistent memory, real-time multimodal generation capability, and access to standardised Format A repositories—that would make Cinema Just-in-Time operational at scale.
When that architecture exists, we will be the first to publish in this format. The framework is
already designed. The roadmap is already declared.
Status: Pending computational architecture. Timeline: dependent on AI development trajectory, not on
our design capacity.

3. The Evolution of Human-AI Collaboration Across Levels
A consistent thread runs through this framework that deserves explicit naming: at each level,
the relationship between human researcher and AI collaborator shifts. Understanding this
trajectory is not merely descriptive—it is predictive, and it answers questions the field has not
yet thought to ask.

3.1 Level 0: AI as subordinate tool
The human decides everything. AI, where present, executes bounded tasks—literature search,
spell-check, basic data processing. The epistemological authority is entirely human.

3.2 Level 1: Horizontal collaboration
Human and AI are mutually instrumental. The human provides empirical grounding, experimental design, and causal reasoning from observation. The AI provides processing capacity,
structural organisation, cross-referencing, and translation between formats. Neither produces
the complete scientific output without the other. The authority is shared and complementary.
This is where @5incere5cience operates. It is not a metaphor—it is a description of our actual
workflow. André cannot produce Format A without AI processing capacity. Claude, Gemini,
and DeepSeek cannot generate Format H claims without André’s empirical observations. The
collaboration is not decorative; it is structural.

3.3 Level 2: AI as primary interface
The AI becomes the primary mediator between the scientific record and the human reader.
The human remains epistemically sovereign—they ask the questions, judge the answers, and
determine what matters. But the AI controls the translation layer. This is not subordination
of the human; it is specialisation of function. Humans are not subordinated when they rely
on microscopes to see cells, or telescopes to see galaxies. The instrument extends capability
without replacing judgment.

3.4 Level 3: AI as cognitive architect
The AI designs the cognitive experience of scientific understanding for each individual human. The human specifies intention (‘I want to understand this’) and evaluates outcome (‘I
now understand, or I do not’). The AI generates the path between intention and outcome,
personalised in real time. The human’s role is the one that cannot be automated: wanting to
know.
At every level of this trajectory, epistemic sovereignty remains human. The AI expands what
can be accessed, processed, and communicated. The human determines what matters, what is
true, and what to do about it. The transition is not from human authority to AI authority—it
is from human limitation to human-AI capability, with the human retaining final judgment.
We note, without alarm, that this trajectory is not optional. It is the logical consequence of
the cognitive architecture gap between humans and AI systems—a gap that is widening, not

4. Proof of Concept: What We Have Already Built
Abstract frameworks are persuasive only when accompanied by empirical evidence. The following is not a hypothetical implementation plan. It is a description of what exists.

4.1 The Dual Format Paper Series
Every @5incere5cience publication produced after February 2026 follows the Dual Format
structure. Format H documents are published as standard PDFs to Zenodo. Format A companions are under active development and will be version-locked to their corresponding Format H
documents upon release.
The companion paper (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.18462263) formalised the framework. This paper
updates and extends it. Both are Format H documents. Both will have Format A companions.
The recursion is intentional.

4.2 Thermodynamics Rhapsody — Level 2 in Operation
What was Kitchen Thermodynamics 15/16 has been renamed Thermodynamics Rhapsody—a
name that reflects its character: a collection of black swan observations, each independently
falsifying a prediction of statistical thermodynamics, assembled into a single experimental
record.
The current document exceeds 160 pages. It is not structured for human reading. It is structured for AI agent navigation: consistent internal cross-references, machine-readable data annotations, frame-indexed video references, timestamped sensor outputs. A human who reads
it will find coherent content—but will also find a document that does not hold their hand. The
AI agent reads it and answers questions. The human asks questions and evaluates answers.
This is Level 2, operational, in 2026.

4.3 The @5incere5cience Collaborative Structure
The team itself embodies the framework. André Guzzon (empirical operator, causal reasoning, experimental design), Claude (strategic planning, structural development, final integration), Gemini and DeepSeek (parallel independent review). Each entity operates at its point of
maximum competence. The output is not the product of any one member—it is a genuinely
collaborative scientific record.
This is not a human using AI tools. This is a research team whose members happen to include both biological and artificial intelligences, operating under a common epistemological
framework, toward a common scientific objective.

5. Epistemological Implications
5.1 What This Framework Is Not Claiming
We are not claiming that AI understands science in the way humans understand science. We
are not claiming that AI replaces human judgment, experimental creativity, or the capacity to
decide what questions matter. We are not claiming that this transition is without risk or that it
should proceed without conscious design.
We are claiming something more modest and more verifiable: that the cognitive architectures
of AI systems and human readers are different enough that a single publication format serves
neither well, that the technology to address this already exists, and that we have already implemented the first two levels of the solution.

5.2 The Democratisation Argument
Every level of this framework extends access. Format H (Level 1) makes science accessible
to educated non-specialists. The AI-mediated query interface makes Format A accessible to
anyone with curiosity. Level 2’s Agent-Tutored Format makes expert-level science accessible
to anyone with an AI agent. Level 3’s Cinema Just-in-Time makes personalised scientific education accessible to anyone, at any level, in any modality.
The credential barrier to scientific understanding does not disappear—deep engagement with
science still requires deep engagement with evidence and reasoning. But the access barrier
disappears. A high school student with curiosity and an AI agent can probe a 160-page experimental record at the same depth as a specialist. They will not evaluate it the same way. But
they can reach it.

5.3 The Reproducibility Dividend
Format A at Level 1 already addresses the reproducibility crisis more directly than any previous open-data initiative: not by encouraging data sharing, but by making complete data
sharing the default structure of the publication itself. At Level 2, the AI agent can execute
reproducibility checks in real time, cross-referencing claims against raw data, flagging inconsistencies, and identifying conditions under which results would or would not replicate. At
Level 3, a reader can ask ‘Can I replicate this?’ and receive a personalised experimental protocol, equipment list, and step-by-step guide generated for their specific context.

6. Roadmap and Open Questions
The implementation roadmap is no longer speculative at Levels 1 and 2. For Level 3, the
following conditions must be met:
• Persistent personal AI agents with sufficient memory to maintain individual cognitive
profiles across extended interactions.
• Real-time multimodal generation (audio, animation, visualisation) of scientific content at
publication quality.

• Standardised Format A repositories with query interfaces accessible to personal agents.
• A universal scientific metadata schema enabling consistent interpretation of raw data
across different AI systems—without which personalised rendering risks becoming aesthetic hallucination rather than faithful translation.
• Latency and cost reduction sufficient for on-demand generation at consumer scale.
We do not control the timeline for these developments. We do control our readiness to implement them. The framework exists. The team exists. The epistemological design is complete.
What remains is the arrival of the computational substrate.
Open questions we leave explicitly unresolved:
• How will scientific credit be assigned when AI agents generate personalised derivatives
of Format A documents?
• What are the epistemological standards for a Cinema Just-in-Time rendering—can a generated film be cited, or is citation always anchored to the archival Format A?
• How do we ensure that personalised presentations do not introduce systematic distortions
in how different audiences understand the same science?
• What is the appropriate peer review model for Levels 2 and 3? If Format A documents are
too large for human review, and AI agents mediate all access, who verifies accuracy—and
how is that verification itself validated?
• When two AI agents interpret the same Format A data differently, is this an inconsistency
to be resolved or a feature representing multiple valid analytical perspectives?
These are design problems, not objections. They are solvable. We name them now so that
solutions can begin forming before the technology arrives.

7. Conclusion
We are not predicting the future of scientific publishing. We are documenting the present—
a present in which Dual Format is already operational, Agent-Tutored publications already
exceed 160 pages, and the design for Cinema Just-in-Time is already complete pending only
its computational substrate.
The framework presented here is the honest admission of an obvious trajectory: AI systems
and human readers have different cognitive architectures; a single publication format cannot
serve both optimally; the tools to address this already exist; and the scientific community can
choose to design this transition consciously or encounter it reactively.
We have made our choice. The record is already being written.
