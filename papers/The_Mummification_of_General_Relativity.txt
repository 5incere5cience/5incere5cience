In normal science, theories die when anomalies accumulate, ad hoc modi cations become elaborate, a new theory explains both successes and failures of the old, and textbooks are updated. This is the normal lifecycle: Ptolemaic astronomy → Copernican heliocentrism, phlogiston → oxidation, Newtonian mechanics → relativity. 
10.2 Why GR Hasn't Died

The Priestly Class: Those who master GR's tensor calculus form an exclusive priest hood with membership requirements: mathematical sophistication, institutional creden tials, demonstrated orthodoxy, and rejection of heterodoxy.
