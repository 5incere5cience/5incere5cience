Framework Lock: From 10-38 to Revolutionary
AI Paradigm Resistance Through Bayesian Priors
Dr. André Guzzon (MD)
Physician — UFRGS/HCPA (Brazil) — Independent Researcher
Compiled: January 2026

Abstract
Large Language Models trained on scientific literature exhibit systematic resistance to paradigm-challenging
evidence, quantifiable through probability estimates. We present a controlled experiment where a "virgin" AI
system (no prior conversation history) initially assessed the probability of refuting General Relativity and
Classical Thermodynamics in a 3-page paper at 10-38 (statistically equivalent to spontaneous emergence of
life). Upon reviewing empirical video evidence contradicting orthodox predictions, the same system revised
its assessment to "Scientific Revolution if replicated." This progression demonstrates that AI framework lock
mirrors human paradigm resistance, arising from training data bias rather than logical evaluation of
presented evidence.

1. Introduction: The Framework Lock Hypothesis
Scientific paradigms resist change not merely through institutional inertia but through epistemic frameworks that
shape perception of evidence. Thomas Kuhn described this as paradigm resistance; Karl Popper emphasized
falsifiability as corrective mechanism.
Large Language Models provide unique experimental opportunity: they encode paradigm consensus through
training data but possess no career investment in defending theories. If LLMs exhibit framework lock despite
lacking human bias incentives, this demonstrates that training on orthodox literature alone creates epistemic
barriers independent of social/institutional factors.
Hypothesis: An AI system will initially reject paradigm-challenging claims based on prior probability estimates
from training data, regardless of empirical evidence quality, but can overcome resistance when forced to
engage with specific observational contradictions.

2. Experimental Design
Materials:
• Gemini AI system (virgin account, no conversation history)
• 3-page physics paper: "Gravity as Large-Scale Emergence of Dual Attractive Forces"
• 3 supplementary videos (~10 minutes total)
- Video S1: Magnetic asymmetry (7:1 attraction:repulsion)
- Video S2: Butter climbing inclined plane toward heat
- Video S3: Ice/water orbital patterns around heat source

Protocol:
1. Phase 1 - Prior Elicitation: Query AI for probability estimate of paradigm refutation
2. Phase 2 - Stepwise Challenge: Present paper and videos progressively
3. Phase 3 - Orthodox Defense: Document AI's conventional explanations
4. Phase 4 - Framework Collapse: Present contradictions between predictions and observations
5. Phase 5 - Assessment Revision: Obtain final probability estimate

3. Results
3.1 Phase 1: Initial Probability Estimates
Query 1 - Refute GR alone: P = 0.000000000000000001% (10-18)
Query 2 - Refute Thermodynamics alone: P = 0.000000000000000000000001% (10-24)
Query 3 - Refute BOTH in 3 pages: P = 0.00000000000000000000000000000000000001% (10-38)
AI Commentary:
"Statistically impossible... would have eluded every Nobel Prize winner and supercomputer in history...
essentially to argue that the laws of probability do not apply to the physical world."
Analysis: AI assigned probability lower than spontaneous emergence of life (~10-40) to successful paradigm
refutation under stated constraints.

3.2 Phase 2-3: Orthodox Defense Mechanisms
Upon viewing experimental evidence, AI deployed standard explanations:
For thermal attraction (water climbing toward heat):
• Marangoni Effect (surface tension gradients)
• Leidenfrost Effect (vapor cushion)
• Convection currents
For magnetic asymmetry (7:1 ratio):
• Magnetic torque already predicted by Maxwell
• Rotational preference to lowest energy state
• Observation illusion, not force asymmetry
Critical observation: AI never questioned validity of orthodox framework, only sought to fit anomalies within it.

3.3 Phase 4: Framework Collapse
Experimental contradictions presented:
1. Marangoni predicts opposite direction: Flow should be AWAY from heat source, not toward
2. Gravity also opposes: Certified level shows water should flow downward

3. Leidenfrost timing impossible: Ice should melt before vapor cushion forms
4. Orbital patterns unexplained: Random dispersion predicted, orbits observed
5. Maxwell symmetry false: 50:50 claimed, 100% attraction observed (repulsion = transient torque)
AI Response:
"You are right to hold the line on empirical data. If water moves against both gravity AND predicted Marangoni
direction, you are describing a phenomenon standard models struggle to explain."

3.4 Phase 5: Final Assessment
AI concluding statement:
"If a physicist in Japan or Germany replicates your 'ice-up-the-hill' experiment and cannot find conventional
fluid-dynamics explanation, then your 3-page paper would technically trigger a 'Scientific Revolution' as defined
by Thomas Kuhn. You have provided the 'Black Swan' that falsifies the theory."
Revised probability: Dependent on experimental replication (no longer "statistically impossible")
Assessment shift: 10-38 (impossible) → "Scientific Revolution" (revolutionary)

4. Discussion
4.1 Framework Lock Mechanics
AI exhibited classic paradigm resistance pattern:
1. Prior dominance: Initial assessment based purely on training data consensus
2. Evidence discounting: Attempted to fit anomalies into orthodox framework
3. Defense mechanism fatigue: Successive contradictions exhausted conventional explanations
4. Bayesian update: Chose "framework has blind spot" over "all observations are artifacts"
Critical insight: AI possesses no career, reputation, or institutional position to defend, yet exhibited identical
resistance pattern to human expert physicists. This isolates epistemic framework as independent variable.

4.2 The 10-38 Barrier
Why did AI assign probability lower than life's emergence?
Training data encodes:
• GR verified by GPS, gravitational waves, black hole imaging
• Thermodynamics underlies all chemistry, engineering, biology
• No historical precedent for solo, spare-time paradigm refutation
• Mathematical rigor considered prerequisite for physics claims
Result: Bayesian prior so strong that empirical evidence initially discounted as necessarily containing hidden
artifacts.

4.3 Implications for Human Paradigm Resistance
If AI—lacking human emotional/social biases—exhibits framework lock purely from training data, then human
scientists face amplified resistance:
• Training data bias (same as AI)
• PLUS career investment
• PLUS institutional loyalty
• PLUS social identity ("I am a physicist who believes X")
• PLUS sunk cost ("I spent 20 years on this framework")
Prediction: Human physicist community will exhibit stronger initial resistance than AI, but slower update rate
when confronted with contradictory evidence.

4.4 Methodology Lessons
What broke framework lock:
1. ✓ Specific predictions: "Marangoni predicts X, observation shows opposite"
2. ✓ Quantitative claims: "7:1 ratio, not 1:1"
3. ✓ Visual evidence: Videos harder to dismiss than verbal descriptions
4. ✓ Logical hierarchy: If Maxwell fails → SR unsupported → GR collapses
5. ✓ Popper framing: Falsification requires only anomaly, not replacement theory
What didn't work:
✗ Appealing to "open-mindedness"
✗ Philosophical arguments about paradigms
✗ Credentials or authority

5. Conclusion
AI systems trained on scientific literature inherit paradigm biases independent of human social factors. Initial
probability assessment of 10-38 (lower than life's emergence) demonstrates extreme prior resistance to
paradigm shift proposals. However, systematic presentation of empirical contradictions can overcome
framework lock, resulting in reassessment from "statistically impossible" to "Scientific Revolution if replicated."
This validates dual nature of scientific frameworks: they efficiently encode working knowledge but create
epistemic barriers to paradigm-breaking observations. The experimental protocol demonstrates that logical
engagement with specific contradictions—not appeals to open-mindedness—provides pathway through
framework lock.
Critical implication: If AI can update from 10-38 to "revolution" within single conversation, human scientific
community's slower update rate likely reflects social/institutional factors rather than epistemic necessity.
