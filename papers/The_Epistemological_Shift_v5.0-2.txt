Popper’s Criterion of Demarcation

Karl Popper established the fundamental distinction between scientific and non-scientific theories not through their truth content, but through their vulnerability to refutation [4]. A theory
which is not refutable by any conceivable event is non-scientific. Irrefutability is not a virtue
but a vice. For Popper, scientific theories must be prohibitive—they must state clearly which
events cannot happen.
A theory is scientific if: (a) it makes specific, risky predictions; (b) it prohibits certain outcomes
from occurring; (c) there exists a Black Swan—a single observation that would refute it. A
theory is non-scientific if: (a) it explains all possible observations; (b) contradictory evidence is
reinterpreted as consistent; (c) it accommodates any outcome through post-hoc rationalization.

A Note on Probabilistic Theories and Popper

Popper accepted probabilistic theories as scientific—provided they define specific thresholds
of improbability that, if violated, would count as falsification. A theory that predicts “event X
will occur with probability 0.99 ± 0.01” is falsifiable: if X occurs only 50% of the time under
controlled conditions, the theory is refuted. Statistical Thermodynamics, however, operates
with “extreme improbability” without ever defining a threshold. No frequency of anomalous
observations is sufficient to trigger falsification, because any deviation is accommodated as
“fluctuation.” This is the critical distinction: probabilistic theories can be scientific; theories
with undefined probability thresholds that accommodate all outcomes cannot.

The Historical Pivot: From Caloric to Statistics

Before the kinetic theory of heat, scientists used the Caloric—a hypothetical invisible fluid flowing from hot to cold bodies. The mathematics worked, but the fluid did not exist. However, the
Caloric theory was prohibitive: it stated that caloric cannot flow from cold to hot without work
input. If observed, the theory was falsified. Despite its fictional ontology, it was empirically
testable—and therefore scientific.
Joule, Clausius, and Boltzmann demonstrated that heat was kinetic energy of molecular motion.
This was a triumph: a microscopic mechanism replaced an invisible fluid.
But here lies the critical juncture—the epistemological shift.
When Caloric was discarded, it was not replaced with a physical force. Instead, Boltzmann
introduced Statistical Mechanics: S = kB ln(W ). The Second Law became: entropy tends to
increase because there are more ways to be disordered than ordered.
Physics traded a fictional substance for a mathematical tautology. The Caloric Theory had a
fictional ontology but a prohibitive, falsifiable nature. Statistical Mechanics has a mathematical ontology and an accommodative, unfalsifiable nature. The tool (mathematics) usurped the
domain of physics (identifying physical causes).

The Formal Proof: Tautology and the Material Conditional

Beyond the experimental evidence, the unfalsifiability of Statistical Thermodynamics can be
demonstrated through propositional logic alone.5
In classical logic, a tautology is a formula that is always true regardless of the truth values of
its components (P ∨ ¬P: “it will rain today or it will not rain today”). A contradiction is always
false (P ∧ ¬P). In the material conditional (P → Q: “if P then Q”), the implication is true
whenever P is false, regardless of Q. The only case that falsifies the conditional is P true and Q
false—this is Popper’s Black Swan.
A scientific theory must define conditions under which P is true and Q is prohibited. If such a
pair (P, Q) exists, the theory can be tested. If no such pair exists, the theory is a tautology—it
accepts any Q for any P.
Statistical Thermodynamics does not postulate any condition as “impossible.” It operates exclusively with “extreme improbability.” For any initial state P, all final states Q are admissible—
some are merely “very rare.” Therefore, there is no pair (P, Q) such that P is true and Q is
prohibited. The structure P → Q never encounters the critical case of P true and Q false. The
theory produces only tautologies.
This is not a philosophical objection. It is a formal logical proof that the statistical formulation
fails the most basic criterion of scientific demarcation. The theory produces only mathematical tautologies: it generates numerical content (predictions of distributions and averages) but
constrains nothing in the physical world, because it prohibits nothing. A theory that never
encounters the case of P true and Q false is logically empty—indistinguishable, by Popper’s
criterion, from non-science.

how a surface tension gradient translates into fluid motion—it is the grammatical “=” connecting a thermal cause to a kinetic consequence. The cause itself—the thermal gradient, and

The promotion is not merely sloppy usage. It constitutes a specific instance of Framework Lock

label that sounds causal, it satisfies the investigator’s demand for explanation before the ontological question—what is actually causing this?—has been posed. The effect’s name functions
as a semantic stopper, terminating the causal chain one link too early.

In each case, the structure is identical: the operator is promoted to cause, and the actual cause—

named effect is not wrong; it is incomplete in a way that masquerades as complete. This is
Framework Lock at its most efficient: not a barrier to investigation, but a sign that reads “investigation complete” placed one step before the destination.

The Double Standard of Evidential Rigor

There is a revealing asymmetry in how evidential standards are applied. When the present
authors claim something as straightforward as Newton’s Third Law—justifying a pan jumping off a stove by the same logic that explains a human jumping by pushing the ground—and
present videos, images, measurements, calculations, graphs, divergences with official theory, a
proposed new equation, and a new falsifiable theory in Popperian terms, the standard response
from the academy is dismissal: colloquial words without logic, without coherence, deployed
as supreme truth. When it is the heretic making a claim, total transparency and millimetric
precision are demanded. When it is the establishment responding, loose words suffice.
This asymmetry extends to the instruments of publication. Where are the video-recorded experiments, conducted with the most precise scientific thermometers, published in Nature, that
established that the liquid receiving heat from below is always hotter at the bottom and cooler
at the surface and cooler still at the liquid–gas interface? Such experiments are not documented
with the transparency demanded of those who challenge the paradigm. The present research
program includes multiple experiments with multiple liquids and multiple types of thermometers, all pointing to a pattern that is the opposite of the official one—experiments that any person
in the world can replicate in an afternoon.
Yet the academy demands faith. One cannot place in Nature the raw collages of real experiments
with links to real videos—unless, perhaps, it comes from a Harvard laboratory, not someone’s
kitchen. In Nature, one must have CGI images and informative graphs, and the reader must
trust that the data are honest—because anyone in such an experiment could round up, and if it

Future Work

The identification of the Named Effect as Epistemic Closure opens a systematic research programme that this paper can only initiate. The following questions are explicitly posed as invitations to further investigation:
How many named effects in physics are currently functioning as causal explanations when they
are, structurally, mediating operators whose upstream cause has never been formally identified?
A systematic survey of the major named effects across thermodynamics, fluid dynamics, optics,
and electromagnetism—applying the Cause → Effect → Consequence distinction developed in

discipline. Preliminary analysis suggests the phenomenon is not exceptional but pervasive: the
naming convention itself is the mechanism that prevents identification of the actual cause, by
providing a label that satisfies the causal question before it has been ontologically answered.
Additionally, the documentation of Framework Lock in AI systems (Section 4.3, footnote)

Conclusion

This paper has demonstrated that the transition from Caloric to Statistics sacrificed falsifiability
for mathematical elegance. The Second Law transformed from a scientific prohibition (heat
cannot flow cold → hot) to a non-scientific accommodation (heat flow cold → hot is unlikely).
By Popper’s criterion, theories that cannot be refuted by any conceivable event are not science.
Three independent lines of evidence converge on this conclusion. The butter experiment dialogue demonstrates that the theory’s defenses are constructed a priori—no experiment is needed
to expose the immunization, because all outcomes are pre-explained. The distilled water experiment demonstrates that when predictions are pre-registered and data contradict them, the system
deploys sequential escape routes until it must deny the validity of its own instruments. The formal logic proof demonstrates that a theory operating with “extreme improbability” rather than
“impossibility” is structurally tautological—it accepts any outcome for any premise. The extended dialogue of Case Study 3 completes the picture: even when the AI system explicitly confirms the absence of causal prohibitions, the abandonment of Popper, and the inapplicability of
