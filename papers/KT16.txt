Experiment 7: The Ice Age — SVE Cryogenic Resilience and Anomalous Volume Contraction 
Experimental Overview 
This experiment documents three interconnected anomalies related to Structured Vesicular Entities (SVEs) and the freezing behavior of water in different container materials. SVEs are macroscopic structures that form spontaneously in still mineral water — distinct from dissolved gas bubbles — and exhibit properties incompatible with any known gaseous phenomenon. Video: youtube.com/shorts/sDOyiIWXbpU 
The experiment is organized into three sub-experiments: (A) SVE formation and comparison with carbonated water, (B) freezing behavior across container materials (glass, copper, plastic), and (C) microscopic characterization of SVEs in liquid water and in ice.
