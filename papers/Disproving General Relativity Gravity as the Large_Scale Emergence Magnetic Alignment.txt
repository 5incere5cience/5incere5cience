Abstract
This experimental physics paper refutes General Relativity (GR) and Thermodynamics by
empirical tests that contradicted its predictions. We demonstrate that GR’s foundational
dependency, Maxwell’s Equations, fails in three-dimensional systems where magnetic
repulsion is geometrically impossible and we provide empirical evidence that heat is a
vectorial attractive force capable of generating negentropy.
Our experiments reveal that "Gravity" is the macroscopic manifestation of two distinct
attractive forces: (1) 3D Magnetic Alignment, which governs solid bodies and precludes
stable repulsion, and (2) Thermal Attraction, demonstrated by the "antigravitational" flow
of fluids toward heat sources. We present a new cosmological framework where elliptical
orbits are resolved not as a product of curved spacetime, but as the dynamic equilibrium
between magnetic alignment (structural lock) and thermal attraction (orbital pull).

To rigorously test the interaction between thermal gradients and gravity, we designed
the "Iceberg Experiment" (Video S3). A stainless-steel pan containing water was inclined and
placed in a freezer, fixing a solid ice block at the lowest gravitational potential. Upon removal, a
ping-pong ball was used to mechanically verify the negative gradient (downward slope) before
and after the experiment to rule out deformation or levelling errors.
The pan was then placed on a stove with a high-intensity flame directed at the highest point of
the incline. The experiment ran for over 18 minutes (The original video has been sped up by
5.0x). Observations:
1. Gravitational Baseline: Initial meltwater obeyed standard mechanics, flowing
downward.
2. Thermal Reversal (Negentropy): As the thermal gradient intensified, the fluid flow
reversed. Water migrated upward against the gravitational vector, effectively "climbing"
the slope to reach the heat source.

3. Thermodynamic Popper’s Falsification: The system maintained a stable dual-state:
violent boiling occurred at the peak (heat source) while the ice block remained solid at
the base, separated by only 15 centimeters and always showing continuity between the
frozen and boiling water.
4. Marangoni effect cannot explain this upward migration: According to surface
tension gradients, fluids flow from regions of low surface tension (high temperature) to
regions of high surface tension (low temperature). In our setup, the Marangoni vector is
directed downward, acting in conjunction with gravity to push the fluid away from the
heat source. The fact that the fluid migrates upward confirms the existence of a third,
dominant vector—Thermal Attraction—capable of overcoming both gravitational
potential and the thermocapillary Marangoni flow.
Conclusion: this behavior violates the Second Law of Thermodynamics. Rather than dissipating
into uniform thermal equilibrium (entropy increase), the thermal gradient actively organized
matter against gravity. The heat source functioned as an attractive ordering force, creating a
low-entropy flow that overpowered gravitational potential.
6.2: Leidenfrost Orbits and the Elliptical Resolution
As water droplets reached the peak thermal gradient, they did not evaporate instantly but entered stable
orbital patterns (Leidenfrost effect). This behavior models the macroscopic mechanics of cosmic systems.
We propose that cosmological orbits are elliptical because they are the product of two competing
attractive forces, not a single gravitational curve:
1. 3D Magnetic Alignment: This force seeks rigid structural locking and alignment (as seen in the
spherical magnet experiments).
2. Thermal Attraction: This force pulls the orbiting body toward the star (the heat source).
An elliptical orbit is the "breathing" pattern of a celestial body oscillating between the magnetic impulse to
align (torque) and the thermal impulse to merge (attraction). This eliminates the need for "initial velocity"
assumptions or "Dark Matter" halos to explain galactic rotation curves.
